How it works, step by step

Your protections

at every step.

This is not a handshake. Every protection from a standard sale applies here. A few terms actually tilt further in your favor.

  • 🏠
    You hold the mortgage until paid in full.
    On every property where you hold the loan, you remain on title. If a payment is missed, the property reverts to you. You keep what was paid and take back the house.
  • 📋
    Every price is locked in writing before we close anything.
    We sign a Master Purchase Agreement covering all 8 properties, with agreed prices and a closing schedule. It is legally binding from day one, before a single key changes hands.
  • 💰
    Cash at every closing. No waiting.
    Once a year, a pair closes and you receive a cash payment at the table. Not a promise. Not a note. Actual money in your account that day.
  • 🔒
    The final payoff is required by the contract.
    At year 7, I must refinance through a bank and pay the remaining balance. If I do not, I am in default. You keep every payment received and reclaim the property.
  • 📅
    All 8 properties transfer within 4 years.
    After year 4, you are no longer a landlord. No repair calls. No tenant issues. No property management. Monthly payments arrive in your account and that is it.
  • ⚖️

    Your financing is documented with a standard payment agreement and security document, the same type any bank uses for a mortgage. Fully enforceable in court.
Questions worth asking

Straight questions.
Straight answers.

Why is the bank price lower than my per-house ask?

Your pair total is honored exactly. The bank side shows a lower recorded purchase price because that's what will appraise and what a bank will actually lend on. The $50k difference per pair gets added to the seller-financed side instead.

So if your ask is $820k for the pair, you still collect $820k — $360k through the bank at closing and $460k on the note I carry. You never give up a dollar of your ask.

And because you're earning 6% interest on the seller-financed balance over 7 years, the monthly checks are meaningfully larger than a 0% note would produce.

When do you receive the final payoff checks?
The seven year clock starts when each pair closes. House pairs sold in Year 1 pay out in Year 8. Pairs from Year 2 pay out in Year 9. This pattern continues through Year 11. You receive four large payments. This schedule spreads your tax liability over several years.
What happens if you cannot refinance?
I would be in default. You have the legal right to take the houses back. You keep every payment I made. I want to avoid that. A buyer who pays for seven years and increases rents has a profile banks like. I have every reason to finish the deal.
What if you need more cash in a specific year?
We can talk about that. I can change the cash amounts at closing. If you need more in Year 1, I can adjust the structure. My goal is to make sure the total income fits your life. We can move numbers around to find a balance.
Why not list all eight houses on the market?
You could do that. It might work for you. Traditional sales mean eight sets of agent commissions and fees. You would pay all your capital gains taxes at once. I offer a single buyer and fixed prices. You skip the agents and spread out the tax bill. This path offers a level of certainty the open market lacks.
What happens to your current mortgages?
The bank loan for the first house in each pair pays off your existing debt. You get the remaining cash. You use that cash to pay off the mortgage on the second house. This leaves the second house free and clear. You then hold the note as the lender. Both of your original loans are gone after we close each pair.
