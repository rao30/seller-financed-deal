How it works

Two homes a year.
Two paychecks a year.

Each year, one pair closes. The first home sells the normal way — a bank pays you cash at closing. On the second home, you hold the loan at 6% interest. Monthly checks come to you for five years, then the buyer refinances and sends the remaining balance in one final payment. (If refinance rates are above 7% at month 60, the buyer can extend up to two more years — your monthly checks just keep coming.)

The mechanics

How the math works.
You collect your full ask.

Two small mechanics make this structure work — both designed so the pair total in your pocket equals exactly what you'd ask for either home on the open market.

01 · The $50k shift

Move $50k from the bank side
to the seller-finance side.

The bank-financed home is recorded $50k lower than your ask so it appraises and the bank will lend on it. The seller-financed home is recorded $50k higher to match. The pair total — and what hits your account — is unchanged.

02 · The 6% wrapper

Principal + interest =
your full ask.

On the seller-financed home, the recorded principal is set lower than your ask, and the 6% interest you collect over 5 years brings the total back up to your full number. Why split it that way? Two practical reasons: the deed records at a price a bank will actually lend against when the buyer refinances, and your CPA can spread the gain across 5 years of installment-sale tax treatment instead of one big capital-gains hit in year one.

Your protections

You are protected
at every step.

  • 🏠
    You hold the mortgage until paid in full.
    On every seller-financed home, if a payment is missed the property reverts to you. You keep what was paid and take back the house.
  • 📋
    Every price is locked in writing before we close.
    A Master Purchase Agreement covers all 8 properties, with agreed prices and a closing schedule, legally binding from day one.
  • 💰
    Cash at every closing — no waiting.
    Once a year, a pair closes and you receive a cash payment at the table. Actual money in your account that day.
  • 🔒
    The final payoff is required by contract.
    Five years after each closing, the buyer must refinance and pay the remaining balance. If not, they're in default and you reclaim the property. (One extension to seven years is allowed only if refinance rates are above 7% — and you keep collecting monthly checks the whole time.)
